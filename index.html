01  How It Works

The Trojan-RNA™ platform
a switch only cancer can flip

Our Trojan-RNA™ carries special engineered motifs that act as a molecular switch, off by default. A cancer-specific protein binds the motif and flips the switch on, driving insertion of the RNA into the genome.

02  The Result

Healthy cells degrade it
Cancer cells become drug factories

The same RNA reaches every cell, but only cancer cells switch it on.

03  What We Unlock

The full potential of potent drugs
limited by toxicity and durability

Unlocked payloads IL-12 (lead) IL-2 IL-15 IFN-β
Barrier: Toxicity

Cancer-specific expression only

Drug is produced only inside the tumor, so potent cytokines like IL-12, too toxic to give systemically, become usable.

Barrier: Short-lived effect

Genomic integration · durable

The payload integrates into the cancer genome for durable local production, instead of a dose that fades within days.

Barrier: Patient selection

One switch · ~50% of cancers

The switch is present across roughly half of human cancers, building patient selection into the biology, and one chassis fits many payloads.
